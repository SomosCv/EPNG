Knoxville Emergency Preparedness Mini-App

Mobile-Friendly · QR Accessible · Offline-Ready Concept · Ready for Knoxville Review
No app store needed Residents open it from a link or QR code.

This is a working proof-of-concept mini-app inspired by Knoxville's Emergency Preparedness Neighborhood Guide. It takes important public preparedness information and makes it easier for residents and neighborhood groups to access on a phone. If Knoxville finds the concept useful, the next step is a small 60-day pilot using one QR code, one placement location, one primary success metric, and no collection of personal data.

Success Defined

The pilot's success will be defined by a specific number of QR code scans during the 60-day pilot period.

300 QR code scans

Target: 300 scans in 60 days.

All other information, including page visits, most-viewed sections, return visits, and usage trends, will be treated as bonus metrics.

Suggested Domain

The pilot can use a simple public destination such as knoxvilleemergency.app, or another City-approved domain.

The domain should be registered in Knoxville's name and included in the final ownership handoff.

  • Mobile-friendly: Designed for quick use on phones.
  • Simple navigation: Key information is organized into easy sections.
  • Offline-ready concept: Important content can remain available in limited-connectivity situations.
  • Quick access: Residents can open it instantly without using an app store.
What Knoxville Receives
  • A mobile-friendly emergency preparedness mini-app based on the guide.
  • Preparedness content organized into sections such as household readiness, ready kit, neighborhood plan, emergency steps, phone numbers, and resources.
  • A dedicated pilot domain and hosting setup.
  • A QR code system for pilot access and scan measurement.
  • Simple analytics focused on QR scans, plus bonus usage information.
  • Launch support, pilot support, and operational handoff documentation.
Privacy Boundaries

The pilot is designed to measure public interest without collecting personal information from residents.

  • No names are collected.
  • No phone numbers or email addresses are collected.
  • No medical, household, demographic, or resident records are collected.
  • The primary measurable action is whether people scan the QR code and open the mini-app.
